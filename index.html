Purpose & Scope

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) establishes the policies, procedures, and requirements governing Virely LLC's engagement of software developers through the Upwork platform. It applies to all contractors hired to build, test, extend, or maintain any Dealsby or Virely platform including Dealsby Reservations, Dealsby Appointments, Dealsby Referrals, Virely Books, Virely Nexus, TheVirelyExperience.com and related landing pages, and Virely Corporate.

This document defines binding requirements for:

  • Task creation, assignment, and acceptance
  • Milestone structure, review, and CEO approval
  • Testing and quality assurance before milestone release
  • Time logging, documentation, and deliverable standards
  • Project tooling: Jira, Otter AI, and Slack
  • Standup calls, communication SLAs, and async updates
  • CEO review and approval workflows
  • Dispute policies and resolution procedures
  • Professional conduct, IP assignment, and offboarding
ℹ️
Applicability
This SOP is binding on all Upwork contractors engaged by Virely LLC, regardless of contract type, engagement duration, or scope. Contractors must acknowledge receipt of and agreement to this SOP prior to commencing work.

No technology platform access, no Jira provisioning, no staging credentials, and no project commencement will occur until the contractor has executed the eSign PDF. This is a hard gate — not a courtesy requirement.

Task Creation & Assignment
4.1 Task Creation (CEO Responsibility)

All tasks are created by the CEO in Jira and communicated to contractors. Tasks must include:

Task ElementRequirement
Task ID (Jira)Auto-assigned by Jira (e.g., DSBY-047). Referenced in all communications, time logs, commits, and file changelogs.
Platform / ModuleSpecific platform and module or area affected.
DescriptionClear narrative of what is to be built or fixed. Includes current behavior (if a bug), expected behavior, and edge cases.
Acceptance CriteriaExplicit, testable conditions that define done. Minimum 3 criteria per task.
Reference FilesProduction-stable baseline file name and any design references or prior conversation links.
PriorityCritical / High / Medium / Low. Critical and High require same-day acknowledgment.
Estimated Hours or Milestone AmountSet at task creation. Material changes require written CEO approval.
Deadline / Due DateSet in Jira. For multi-day tasks, an interim check-in date is included.
4.2 Task Acceptance (Contractor Responsibility)
  1. Acknowledge every assigned Jira task via Upwork message within 4 business hours. Move ticket to In Progress in Jira.
  2. Confirm understanding of acceptance criteria — ask clarifying questions in the Jira ticket comments or #dev-conversations.
  3. Flag scope conflicts, technical blockers, or estimated-hour overruns immediately upon discovery.
  4. Never begin work on a task that has not been formally assigned in Jira by the CEO.
  5. Never expand task scope without written CEO approval.
🚫
Zero-Scope-Creep Policy
Contractors are prohibited from modifying any code, UI, or logic outside the defined task scope without explicit CEO approval. Unauthorized changes discovered during review will result in rejection of the milestone and may be grounds for contract dispute or termination.

Milestone Structure, Review & Approval
5.1 Milestone Design Principles
  • Fixed-price contracts use milestones of no more than 5 business days in duration.
  • Each milestone represents a discrete, testable deliverable — not a percentage of effort.
  • Each milestone has its own acceptance criteria defined in Jira.
  • Full testing and review cycle required before payment release.
5.2 Milestone Sizing Guidelines
Milestone TypeMaximum Value / Duration
Single feature addition$300 / 5 business days
Bug fix or patch$150 / 2 business days
Platform module (multi-feature)$500 / 7 business days (requires CEO pre-approval)
Full platform build or major refactorMust be broken into sub-milestones; no single milestone > $500
Hourly task rollupReviewed weekly; logged hours validated before approval
5.3 Milestone Submission Requirements
  1. Deliverable File(s): Named per versioning convention; submitted via Upwork file upload.
  2. Submission Summary: Posted in Upwork messages. Must include Task IDs, summary of changes, files modified, known limitations, and test results summary.
  3. Testing Documentation: Completed test checklist submitted as a separate document.
  4. Time Log (hourly): Screenshot or export of logged hours for the milestone period.
  5. Change Log Entry: Brief changelog note added to the deliverable file's internal header comment.
  6. Jira Update: All completed Jira tickets moved to the Done / In Review column.
  7. Slack Notification: Post [MILESTONE SUBMISSION] in #dev-conversations tagging @CEO.
5.4 CEO Review Process
DecisionMeaningNext Step
✅ APPROVEDAll acceptance criteria metMilestone released for payment. CEO posts confirmation in Upwork and #dev-conversations.
⚡ APPROVED WITH CONDITIONSMinor issues notedPayment released after contractor acknowledges conditions and commits to fix in next milestone.
❌ REJECTEDAcceptance criteria not metNo payment. Contractor must address all rejection notes and resubmit with revision summary.
⚠️
Milestone Rejection Policy
A rejected milestone is not grounds for partial payment. If rejected twice for the same issues, the CEO reserves the right to reassign and terminate the contract. Contractors may not open a payment dispute on a formally rejected milestone.
5.5 Revision Rounds
RoundPolicy
First ResubmissionIncluded in original milestone. No additional charge.
Second ResubmissionIncluded only if first rejection was due to unclear CEO acceptance criteria (CEO acknowledges in writing).
Third+ ResubmissionIndicates systemic issue. CEO may terminate the milestone, reassign, and initiate dispute resolution.
5.6 Complete Module Requirement
🚫
Frontend + Backend Integration Required for Approval
Milestone approvals are based on complete, integrated modules. A milestone will not be approved and payment will not be released for partial implementations. Every milestone must deliver a fully functional, integrated module where the complete user flow — from UI interaction through backend logic, data handling, and response — has been implemented, connected, and validated.
5.7 Developer Removal for Substandard Performance
Grounds for Immediate RemovalDescription
Repeated incomplete modulesTwo or more milestone submissions consisting of incomplete or non-integrated modules within a single contract period.
Repeated untested workSubmitting milestones without real-device testing evidence on two or more occasions.
Misrepresentation of skill setContractor represented expertise that deliverables demonstrate does not exist.
Misrepresentation of experiencePortfolio work or credentials provided during pre-hire do not accurately represent actual experience.
Inability to complete tasksAfter two milestone cycles, contractor demonstrates inability to complete tasks to required quality standards.

CEO Review & Final Approval Workflow
  1. Contractor submits deliverable with all required documentation and updates Jira to In Review.
  2. Contractor posts [MILESTONE SUBMISSION] to #dev-conversations and sends Upwork message.
  3. CEO acknowledges receipt within 1 business day.
  4. CEO conducts functional review on real devices (up to 5 business days).
  5. CEO posts decision (APPROVED / APPROVED WITH CONDITIONS / REJECTED) to Upwork, Slack, and updates Jira.
  6. If APPROVED: Upwork milestone released for payment.
  7. If REJECTED: CEO posts itemized rejection notes. Contractor has 48 hours to acknowledge and provide remediation timeline.

17
Scope Change Management & Project Modifications
⚠️
No Unilateral Scope Changes — By Either Party
All project scope changes require the explicit written agreement of both the developer and the CEO before any work on the changed scope may begin. No verbal approvals of any kind. Otter AI meeting transcripts do not constitute binding Change Order approval without written follow-up confirmation.
17.3 Change Order Process
  1. Either party identifies a scope change and raises it in [QUESTION] or [FYI] in #dev-conversations and comments it in the relevant Jira ticket.
  2. The requesting party documents in writing: (a) what is changing, (b) why it is changing, (c) estimated time/cost impact, (d) impact on current milestone deadline.
  3. CEO reviews and responds in writing within 2 business days.
  4. If approved: CEO issues a written Change Order confirmation in Upwork messages and creates an amended Jira ticket. Contractor must then explicitly acknowledge and accept in writing before proceeding.
  5. If denied: Original scope stands. Contractor proceeds with original task only.

Subcontracting Policy

Subcontracting is prohibited by default. Work must be performed by the contracted individual. Any exception requires 5 business days written advance notice, CEO written approval, and the subcontractor must execute the same NDA. The primary contractor remains fully responsible for all subcontracted work.

🚫
Prohibited Subcontracting
Prohibited under all circumstances: using AI-generated code as the primary delivery mechanism without disclosure; using a third party to complete Upwork skill tests or identity verification; subcontracting to individuals in OFAC-sanctioned countries; subcontracting the entire engagement while the primary contractor performs no substantive work.
20
Intellectual Property Assignment

All work product created under Virely LLC engagements is work-for-hire. Upon payment for any milestone, all IP rights transfer to and vest exclusively in Virely LLC. This covers all source code, design assets, documentation, and ideas conceived during the engagement.

  • Contractors may not withhold, delete, or encrypt any deliverable as leverage in a payment dispute — doing so constitutes a material breach.
  • Contractors must disclose all OSS components; GPL-licensed code requires explicit CEO written approval before inclusion.
  • Contractors may not use Virely LLC platform code, screenshots, or descriptions in public portfolios without written CEO approval.

Conflict of Interest

For the duration of any active engagement with Virely LLC, contractors may not work for any direct competitor (reservation management, appointment scheduling, or referral marketing SaaS for small businesses), build any platform substantially similar to any Dealsby product, or repurpose knowledge from Virely LLC in competitor work.

Performance Improvement Plan (PIP)
26.1 PIP Triggers
  • Two milestones rejected in a 30-day period.
  • Three or more communication SLA failures in a 30-day period.
  • Two consecutive missed standup calls without documented notice.
  • Three or more late Jira task deliveries in a 30-day period.
  • Repeated time log non-compliance on 3+ entries in a week.
⚠️
PIP Exemption — Immediate Termination Cases
A PIP is not required for: time log fraud, unauthorized subcontracting, breach of confidentiality, going dark, scope violation, or any material breach. These are grounds for immediate termination.

Liability & Indemnification

Contractors' liability to Virely LLC for any single incident arising from their work is limited to the total amount paid under the relevant contract. This limitation does not apply to willful misconduct, fraud, breach of confidentiality or NDA obligations, IP infringement, or data breaches caused directly by contractor's negligence.

28
Professional Conduct & Developer Interaction
28.1 Core Conduct Principles

Respect: Treat every team member with dignity, regardless of experience level or role. Never use condescending, dismissive, or hostile language.

Professionalism: Maintain a calm and constructive tone in all technical discussions. Keep all feedback specific, actionable, and solution-oriented.

Collaboration: Approach technical disagreements as a shared problem to solve, not a debate to win. Document agreed-upon decisions.

28.3 Handling Technical Disagreements
  1. Direct Discussion: Developers first attempt to resolve the disagreement themselves in a calm, structured conversation based on technical merit.
  2. Document the Disagreement: If step 1 fails, each developer prepares a brief written summary of their proposed approach, technical reasoning, and trade-offs.
  3. Escalate to the CEO: All unresolved technical disagreements are escalated to the CEO. The CEO's decision is final.
🚫
Prohibited Conduct — Grounds for Immediate Termination
Using demeaning or harassing language; publicly ridiculing a fellow developer's technical approach; deliberately withholding information to undermine a colleague's work; continuing to advocate against a decision after the CEO has rendered a final ruling; retaliating against a developer who escalated a disagreement.
28.6 No Promise of Future Contract Opportunities

Virely LLC makes no representations, guarantees, or promises regarding future contract opportunities, renewals, or continued engagement beyond the current active contract. The awarding of any future work is solely at the discretion of the CEO.

28.9 No Solicitation of Reviews or Ratings

Contractors are strictly prohibited from soliciting, requesting, or encouraging the CEO or any Virely LLC associate to leave a 5-star review, positive rating, or any public endorsement on Upwork or any other platform — at any time during or after the engagement.

Purpose & Scope

This document establishes the official application and selection process that all software developers — both prospective and currently engaged — must follow in order to be considered for, and awarded, active software development projects at Virely Smart Marketing Technologies (Virely LLC).

This process applies to all Virely platforms, including:

  • Dealsby Reservations
  • Dealsby Appointments
  • Dealsby Referrals
  • Virely Books
  • Virely Nexus
  • TheVirelyExperience.com and related landing pages
  • Virely.co and all affiliated web properties
  • Any future Virely platform or internal tooling project
⚠️
Important
Being actively engaged on a current Virely development project does not guarantee consideration for, or selection to, future projects. Each project opportunity requires a separate application, evaluation, and selection decision.
2
Key Governing Policies

All applicants and engaged developers must comply with the following:

2.1 Virely Contractor Developer Engagement Policy

All developers are required to read, understand, and adhere to the Virely Contractor Developer Engagement Policy in its entirety. This policy governs professional conduct, confidentiality, intellectual property ownership, communication standards, deliverable quality expectations, and termination conditions for all contractor engagements at Virely.

2.2 Deel Platform Enrollment

All developers — regardless of experience with Virely or contractor status — must be enrolled and in good standing on the Deel platform before they can be awarded any project. Deel is Virely's exclusive platform for contractor onboarding, contract execution, and payment processing.

Developers who are not yet registered on Deel must complete registration and onboarding before or during the application process. Project awards will not be issued until Deel enrollment is confirmed.

ℹ️
Deel Enrollment Requirements
  • Valid Deel contractor account created and verified
  • W-8BEN or W-9 tax form completed as applicable
  • Banking/payment method connected and active
  • Identity verification completed within Deel
3
Eligibility Requirements

To be eligible to apply for a Virely development project, all applicants must meet the following minimum requirements:

3.1 General Eligibility
  • Must be 18 years of age or older
  • Must be legally authorized to work as an independent contractor in your country of residence
  • Must have an active, verified Deel contractor account (or complete enrollment before project start)
  • Must not have any outstanding policy violations, unresolved deliverable disputes, or active termination proceedings with Virely
  • Must agree to all terms outlined in the Virely Contractor Developer Engagement Policy
3.2 Technical Eligibility

Technical eligibility is assessed on a per-project basis. Applicants will be evaluated on the specific skills required for each open project. Common technical qualifications include:

  • Demonstrated proficiency in the relevant programming languages, frameworks, or platforms listed in the project posting
  • Portfolio or code samples demonstrating relevant prior work
  • Verifiable experience with similar project types (SaaS platforms, Next.js, Nest, HTML/CSS/JS, backend APIs, mobile, etc.)
  • Ability to work independently with minimal supervision
  • Reliable development environment, tools, and internet connectivity
ℹ️
Note
Current Virely developers are not exempt from technical eligibility requirements. All applicants compete on skill and relevance, not tenure.
4
Application Process

The Virely Software Development Project Application Process consists of five stages. All applicants — new and current — must complete each applicable stage.

Applications are accepted only during the open window stated in the project posting. Per posting

4 Technical Interview Qualified applicants — including currently engaged Virely developers — will participate in a structured technical interview. The interview evaluates skills directly relevant to the open project, problem-solving ability, and familiarity with the Virely tech ecosystem. Scheduled per applicant
5 Selection & Award Virely selects the most qualified candidate(s) based on interview performance, relevant project experience, portfolio quality, and availability. All applicants are notified of the outcome. Selected developers receive a formal project agreement via Deel. Within 5 business days of interviews
5
Interview Requirements

All applicants who pass initial screening are required to participate in a technical interview. This requirement applies equally to new applicants and to developers who are currently working on active Virely projects.

5.1 Interview Format
  • Duration: Approximately 45–60 minutes
  • Format: Video call via a Virely-approved platform (Google Meet, Zoom, or equivalent)
  • Participants: At least one Virely technical evaluator and/or the Virely CEO
  • Content: Technical skill assessment, project-specific questions, and a brief code/logic exercise relevant to the open project
5.2 Interview Evaluation Criteria
  • Technical proficiency in skills required for the project
  • Demonstrated understanding of the Virely platform ecosystem (for current developers)
  • Code quality, problem-solving approach, and communication clarity
  • Ability to deliver independently within defined timelines
  • Professionalism and alignment with Virely's standards and values
5.3 Re-Interview Policy

Developers who have previously completed a Virely interview are not exempt from re-interviewing for new projects. Each project represents a distinct engagement with its own unique technical requirements. Past interview performance does not substitute for current evaluation.

7
Project Award & Onboarding
7.1 Project Award

Selected developers will receive a formal project award notification and a project-specific contract via the Deel platform. The contract will include:

  • Project scope and deliverable definitions
  • Milestone schedule and associated payment amounts
  • Start and expected completion dates
  • Hourly rate or fixed-project compensation terms
  • IP assignment and confidentiality provisions
  • Reference to the Virely Contractor Developer Engagement Policy
7.2 Contract Execution

Developers must sign the project contract via Deel within 48 hours of receiving the award notification. Failure to execute the contract within this window may result in the award being rescinded and the opportunity offered to the next qualified candidate.

7.3 Project Kickoff

Upon contract execution, the Virely project lead will schedule a project kickoff call to review scope, establish communication cadence, confirm access to required resources, and align on delivery milestones.

8
Policy for Currently Engaged Developers

Developers currently working on an active Virely project are welcome and encouraged to apply for additional or future project opportunities. However, the following policies apply:

  • Current engagement does not grant priority, preference, or exemption in the application process for future projects
  • All currently engaged developers must complete the full application and interview process for any new project they wish to be considered for
  • Concurrent project engagement is permitted only if explicitly approved in writing by Virely and provided that existing project commitments are not negatively impacted
  • Developers must maintain satisfactory performance on all active projects throughout the duration of any application process for additional work
  • A developer on a Performance Improvement Plan (PIP) or under active dispute resolution is ineligible to apply for new projects until the matter is resolved
ℹ️
Virely evaluates each project independently. Past contributions, however valued, do not substitute for demonstrated current readiness to deliver on new project requirements.

  • Email: Official Virely team email address on file with Deel
  • Deel Platform: Contract delivery, payment, and official engagement documentation
  • Virely Developer Portal: Project postings, announcements, and submission forms (when active)

All applicants will receive a written decision — acceptance or rejection — within the timeline specified in the project posting. Virely does not provide detailed feedback to unsuccessful applicants, though general guidance may be offered at Virely's discretion.

10
Disqualification & Appeals
10.1 Grounds for Disqualification

Applicants may be disqualified from consideration at any stage of the process for the following reasons:

  • Submission of false, misleading, or inaccurate information in the application
  • Failure to complete Deel enrollment prior to project award
  • Violation of the Virely Contractor Developer Engagement Policy
  • Unprofessional conduct during the interview or any stage of the process
  • Active dispute, termination proceeding, or unresolved deliverable issues with Virely
  • Failure to respond to scheduling or communication requests within stated timeframes
10.2 Appeals

Applicants who believe they were disqualified in error may submit a written appeal to Virely's People & Talent Operations team within 5 business days of receiving their disqualification notice. Appeals must include the applicant's name, the project applied for, and a clear description of the grounds for the appeal. Virely's decision on appeals is final.
